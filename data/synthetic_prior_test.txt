ITU-T Recommendation Q.3960 — SIM Box Fraud Detection Guidelines
================================================================

Section 3.2: Causal Mechanisms in Telecom Revenue Assurance

The following causal relationships have been identified through operator
experience and regulatory analysis:

1. An increase in CallDataRecordVolume directly causes higher NetworkLoad
   on core switching infrastructure. This relationship is well-established
   across multiple operator datasets (confidence > 0.90).

2. SIMBoxFraudAttempts have a direct causal effect on RevenueLeakageVolume.
   Each percentage increase in SIM box fraud attempts leads to a proportional
   increase in revenue leakage, typically with a 2-4 hour lag.

3. FraudPolicyStrictness directly improves SIMFraudDetectionRate. Operators
   who implement stricter fraud policies see measurably higher detection rates
   within the first billing cycle.

4. RevenueLeakageVolume has a strong causal impact on CashFlowRisk. Revenue
   leakage erodes cash reserves and increases financial exposure.

5. SIMFraudDetectionRate causally reduces ARPUImpact. Higher detection rates
   mitigate the negative impact on Average Revenue Per User.

6. RegulatorySignal from national regulators intensifies ITURegulatoryPressure
   on operators to adopt fraud mitigation measures.

7. NetworkLoad contributes to increased NetworkOpExCost through additional
   infrastructure provisioning requirements.

Section 4.1: Secondary Effects

8. SubscriberRetentionScore is influenced by ARPUImpact. Subscribers exposed
   to degraded service quality (reflected in lower ARPU metrics) show higher
   churn propensity.

Note: These relationships were validated through field studies conducted by
ITU member administrations across 15 developing markets (2022-2025).
